Who We Are

Insuring Opportunity

Gamma Iota Sigma (ΓΙΣ) is a college academic fraternity, founded on April 16, 1966 at The Ohio State University in Columbus, Ohio.

We are organized to promote, encourage, and sustain student interest in insurance, risk management, and actuarial science as professions. We aim to encourage high moral and scholastic attainments and to facilitate the interaction and cooperation of educational institutions, industry, and professional organizations by fostering research, scholarship, and improved public relations.

"Gamma Iota Sigma is recognized as the insurance industry's premier collegiate talent pipeline."

— GIS National
Career Paths

Where a Career
in Insurance
Takes You

The insurance and risk management industry offers a diverse range of career paths — from analytics-heavy actuarial roles to client-facing sales positions — all with strong compensation and growth potential.

Actuarial / Accounting
Actuarial
Median: $125,770

Actuaries predict risk using analytical and financial accounting skills, weighing variables like age and statistical data. Strong math background essential.

Actuarial / Accounting
Internal Auditing
Median: $95,000

Auditors monitor and streamline operations across all areas of business, developing strategies and controls to maximize productivity and meet company objectives.

Actuarial / Accounting
Controller
Median: $110,000

The top accounting management role — guides financial decisions for executives and the board, monitors overall financial health of the company.

All Business
Claims
Median: $73,400

Claims professionals fulfill the insurance promise. Roles range from office-based analysts to field-based adjusters who examine losses on site.

All Business
Risk Management
Median: $90,000

Risk managers prevent losses before they occur — a broad field closely linked to insurance, typically requiring five or more years of related experience.

All Business
Underwriting
Median: $77,860

Underwriters create insurance policies using complex algorithms and risk techniques — foundational to every corner of the industry.

Marketing / Sales
Sales — Agents & Brokers
Median: $60,370

Two main types: agents sell on behalf of specific companies; brokers connect buyers with carriers across the market. Both spend most of their time networking and closing deals with flexible, client-facing schedules.
