Who We Are

Insuring Opportunity

Gamma Iota Sigma (ΓΙΣ) is a college academic fraternity, founded on April 16, 1966 at The Ohio State University in Columbus, Ohio.

We are organized to promote, encourage, and sustain student interest in insurance, risk management, and actuarial science as professions. We aim to encourage high moral and scholastic attainments and to facilitate the interaction and cooperation of educational institutions, industry, and professional organizations by fostering research, scholarship, and improved public relations.

"Gamma Iota Sigma is recognized as the insurance industry's premier collegiate talent pipeline."

— GIS National

Career Paths

Where a Career in
Insurance Takes You

The insurance and risk management industry offers a diverse range of career paths — from analytics-heavy actuarial roles to client-facing sales positions — all with strong compensation and growth potential.

Actuarial / Accounting
Actuarial
Median: $125,770

Actuaries employ analytical skills and financial accounting knowledge to predict the risk associated with writing insurance policies, weighing factors like age and other variables. Strong math and statistical analysis skills are essential.

Actuarial / Accounting
Internal Auditing
Median: $95,000

Internal auditors monitor and streamline operations across all areas of business, developing strategies and controls to maximize productivity, attract customers, grow the business, and meet company objectives and goals.

Actuarial / Accounting
Controller
Median: $110,000

The controller acts as the top management person in the accounting department, guiding financial decisions made by executives and the board of directors while monitoring the overall financial condition of the company.

All Business
Claims
Median: $73,400

Claims professionals fulfill the insurance promise — when something bad happens, they ensure customers are compensated. Roles range from office-based claims clerks and analysts to field-based claims adjusters who examine losses on site.

All Business
Risk Management
Median: $90,000

Risk managers analyze and implement ways to prevent losses before they occur. It's a broad field closely linked to insurance, and typically requires five or more years of experience in related fields like insurance or loss control consulting.

All Business
Underwriting
Median: $77,860

Underwriters create insurance policies — they decide what should be covered and how much to charge. Using complex algorithms and risk management techniques, they work behind the scenes across every corner of the industry.

Marketing / Sales
Sales
Median: $60,370

Two main types: agents (who sell on behalf of specific companies) and brokers (who connect buyers with carriers across the market). Both spend most of their time networking and closing deals, with flexible hours and client-facing roles.
